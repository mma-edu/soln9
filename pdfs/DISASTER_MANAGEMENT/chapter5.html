EXERCISES
Q1. Prepare a school disaster management plan for various hazards like fire, earthquake,
floods and cyclones. The class teacher can divide students into groups and help in
preparing the disaster management plan.
Ans:- The school disaster management plan can be prepared along the following
lines:
(i) To study school building and its surroundings to find out whether it is safe or not,
(ii) To find out whether the fire extinguishers are properly working and filled at least
once every year,
(iii) To examine whether there are heavy shelves that may fall down during an
earthquake,
(iv) To find out whether the ambulance or fire brigade can reach the school premises
easily or not ,
(v) To see whether the school is near a river and can be flooded easily or not,
(vi) To examine whether the school has strong and high walls or trees to mitigate the
effect of a cyclonic storm.
Q2. What can you do as a student towards the community for disaster management?
Ans:- Student can facilitate planning for community disaster risk reduction schemes. They
can run the school Disaster Management Committee and impart practical training to the
community about disaster risk reduction skills through the Committee. Students can
become trained community members in the matter of disaster management and they should
always remember that without their education and involvement, no disaster management
plan cloud be competed.

EXTRA QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS
Q1. What is the aim of community planning for disaster management?
Ans:- The aim of community planning for disaster management is to reduce the
vulnerabilities and increase the capacities of the individuals and the community to cope
with the damaging effects of disasters.
Q2. How can community disaster management develop local coping mechanisms?
Ans:- Since disasters are recurrent, there is always a traditionally established coping
mechanism that is handed down over the generation. This would be the most immediate
response measure for the local environment.
Q3. What is the stand of the Government of Indian about community planning for
disaster management?
Ans:- The Government of India strongly advocates the building of community capacities
in various aspects of disaster management.
Q4. Why do different communities share some commonality?
Ans:- It is because they might be living in the same environment exposed to hazards and
having been affected by a disaster.
Q5. State five reasons as to why the community should be at the head of any disaster
management plan?
Ans:- The reasons are :
(i) The community is the first responder, since it is at the site of the disaster,
(ii) It is the source of maximum information for the people living there,
(iii) The community can provide the local coping mechanisms,
(iv) It proves that self-help is in self-interest because dependence on external help may be
time-consuming and even less effective,
(v) Community involvement helps in creating a less vulnerable community.
