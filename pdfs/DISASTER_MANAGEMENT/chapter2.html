EXERCISES
Q1. What common man-made hazard can you identify in your city or area? Which of
them according to you may result in a disaster, if not adequately addressed through
prevention, mitigation and preparedness activities?
Ans:- Lack of sanitary latrines and proper sewage facilities may cause epidemics or
environmental degradation. Improper electrical wiring may also lead to electric short
circuits which may cause house burning. Leakage of toxic gases from factories, improper
location of industrial units and plants, use of chemical fertilizers and pesticides in
agriculture etc. may result in a disaster, of not adequately addressed through prevention,
mitigation and preparedness activities.
Q2. Visit a nearby Fire Station and request a fire-fighter to explain how to distinguish
between different types of fires.
Ans:- There are different types of fire. It may be domestic fire or wild fire. It may be fire
through human activities or fire through natural factors like lightning. Again, it may be an
accidental fire or a deliberate fire. Further, it may also be on a small scale involving only
one house or a shop, or a larger scale, involving several contiguous houses or shops. Fire
from burning oil, industrial fire, fire caused by electric short circuit, forest fires etc. are
some common types of fires.

EXTRA QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS
Q1. What is the first step to be taken in disaster management?
Ans:- The first step to be taken in disaster management is Preparedness, which means that
the communities and services are capable of coping with effects of a disaster to facilitate
repaid response.
Q2. What are the measures taken during the Response phase of disaster management?
Ans:- During the Response phase of disaster management, the measures taken are the
Government and Non-Government Organisations provide foods, clothing and temporary
shelter to those affected by the disaster.
Q3. What are the measures taken during the Recovery phase of disaster management?
Ans:- During the Recovery phase, the affected people reconstruct their homes and
essential services like reconstruction of roads, bridges, schools etc. are restored.
Q4. What are the measures taken during the Prevention phase of disaster management?
Ans:- During the Prevention phase, actions are carried out to minimize the impact of
potential risk.
Q5. What is the full form of PRRP?
Ans:- Preparedness, Response, Recovery and Prevention/Planning.
